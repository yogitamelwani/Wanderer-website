More about Shimla!

Shimla - The Summer Capital of Himachal Shimla Himachal Pradesh, one of the most well-liked hill villages in northern India and the old summer capital of British India, is a favourite choice among newlyweds. Shimla, commonly referred to as the "Queen of Hill Stations," sits at an impressive 2,202 metres above sea level. Himachal Pradesh's summer capital, Shimla, was first the provincial capital of Punjab following the country's independence. If you wonder whats Shimla famous for, then all you need to do is get a view of the Himalayas from here. Best Time to Visit Shimla From October through February, Shimla has a cold season. This incredible metropolis experiences lows of -2 degrees Celsius. You can witness snowfall during December and January here and explore the charming places to visit in Shimla. In the months of July through September, the city enjoys the onset of the monsoon season. Travelling to Shimla famous places in the summer season is ideal, and the season starts from March and lasts till June. During this time, Shimla weather turns pleasant. How to Reach the Places to Visit in Shimla The Jubbarhatti Airport, 22 kilometres from Shimla, provides convenient air travel to many different cities. It's simple to go to other airports in the area, such as the ones in Chandigarh and Delhi. Kalka Railway Station is the nearest station, and it links the city to the rest of the country through train. From major cities like Delhi and Calcutta, there are plenty of trains to choose from. There are frequent road connections between Shimla tourist places and other major Indian towns including Chandigarh, Dehradun, and Delhi. Both taxis and state buses will take you to the hill station as well as tourist places near Shimla. What are the things to see and do in Shimla? There are several things to do in Shimla. The Ridge is an open region between the Lakkar Bazaar and the Scandal Point where visitors may get spectacular views of the surrounding mountain peaks. Located at an impressive 20,268 metres above sea level, the Jakhu Temple is devoted to the Hindu God, Hanuman. Christ Church, a masterpiece by Colonel J. T. Boileau, is perched atop the Ridge.

Restaurants in Shimla The Restaurant, Oberoi Cecil You will enjoy every aspect of this Shimla restaurant, from the cosy booths to the elegant main hall and private dining rooms illuminated by chandeliers. A wide variety of Indian and other cuisines are featured. Himachali Rasoi The cuisine is served here in a communal style with rice at the centre and a variety of dishes arranged around it; the titles of the dishes are quite creative. The Open Garden Terrace Restaurant The Open Garden Terrace Restaurant features a fantastic bar and an exquisite multi-cuisine menu. People prefer Thai and Asian meals in this restaurant. Nightlife in Shimla Sixteen 69 Lounge Bar Inside the Marina Hotel on Mall Road is where you'll find the Sixteen 69 Lounge Bar. If you like to get your groove on to songs by famous DJs, this is the place to be. The Pub The Pub, located in Shimla's Ritz Cinema, serves up some of the city's finest drinks. You should definitely sample some of the brand-new drinks and dishes they have added to the menu. Rendezvous Bar You can get a wide range of beverages at the Rendezvous Bar. Professional bartenders serve up inventive cocktails to complement the tasty and crispy bar snacks. The upbeat Punjabi music is the ideal accompaniment to the delicious meal. Places to Stay in Shimla The Oberoi Cecil The Oberoi Cecil, Shimla was constructed during the British era and now provides guests with a magnificent stay complete with elegant accommodations, high-end services, and breathtaking views of the surrounding valley. Radisson Jass Shimla This five-star hotel is unique in its opulence, with its majestic suites, exquisite restaurant, and breathtaking vistas of the Himalayas. The Orchid Shimla This hotel in the mountains provides everything a family might want for a relaxing and enjoyable vacation: a pool, a play area for the kids and a spa. Food to Try in Places to Visit in Shimla In Himachal Pradesh, people often eat the same sorts of North Indian fare that you would find anywhere else in the country. Also, some delicacies carry Tibetan touch. When compared to other states, the population here likewise favours non-vegetarian fare. Some of the Shimla famous foods include madra, sidu, dhaam, thukpa, babru and chha gosht. Things to Buy in the Places to Visit in Shimla Shops in many sections of town guarantee that residents can buy fresh produce and stylish furnishings at prices within their budgets. Tourists of all ages and tastes will find shopping in Shimla a wonderful experience. Lakkar Bazaar Shimla famous things available in Lakkar Bazaar are made of wood. There are artisanal items, historical relics, and even kitchen gadgets among them. Lower Bazaar The brightly coloured ceramics, gloves, shawls, incense sticks, lamps, etc., that can be found in this market are what really set it apart. Himachal emporium The Shimla area emporium is stocked to the gills with gorgeous garments of all kinds, including women's apparel, unusual crockery, hand-made jewellery, stunning Kinnauri shawls, and one-of-a-kind woollen goods. If vacaying in a hill station is on your cards, you cannot miss considering going to the queen of hill stations Shimla. With alluring landscapes, scow clad mountain peaks, beautiful lakes and mesmerising tourist spots, this place has more than enough attractions to offer you on a vacation. Enjoy the touristy crowd or indulge in some steamy momos at a pleasant caf on the Mall road. Spend a day shopping and socializing at the Ridge with a picturesque view of the snow-capped mountains. If you want to explore a less-explored, colder and more scenic place in Shimla, visit Kufri. It is famous for its beautiful landscapes, fun adventurous activities at the Mashru Peak and rare mountain animals at the zoological park. Trek to the popular Jakhoo temple at the Jakhoo Peak, Shimlas highest point, and enjoy the astounding view of the Shivalik mountain range. Appreciate some artistic British-era architecture at Shimlas oh-so-famous Christ Church standing tall amidst serene landscapes. Experience the charm of a township in the lap of the mountains at the Summer Hills, or enjoy a game of golf amidst the lush green meadows of Annandale. Cherish an enthralling view of the Chadwick Waterfall surrounded by the tall forests of deodar and pine trees. Feel proud on a visit to the highest cricket pitch in the world at Chail. You can also witness the outstanding architecture of the Chail Palace. Get a Bollywood-kind feel at the serene greenery of the Green Valley. This place has gained a lot of popularity off-late upon being featured on many Bollywood movies. Among other things to do, you can enjoy a ropeway ride from Jakhu temple, visit the magnificent Kamna Devi Temple, or meet wild animals at the Shimla Reserve Forest Sanctuary. Sounds exciting? Conclusion

As you explore its intricately carved architectural structures and relax in its plush hotels, you will feel a sense of perfection and tranquillity.
