More about Delhi!

Delhi - The Vibrant Capital Of India
Delhi is not only one of the major cities in India but also a fascinating realm that blends ancient and contemporary elements. The nation's capital, Delhi, is India's second-most populated city after Mumbai. Delhi is split into two parts with one being Old Delhi and the other being New Delhi India.

Delhi's culture is quite diversified, much like the city's past. There are various festivals celebrated here including Diwali, Holi, Mahavir Jayanthi, Krishna Janmashtami, Lohri and Guru Nanak Jayanthi.

In addition to historical monuments and tourist places in Delhi, you can also find several modern cafes and restaurants here.

Best Time to Visit Delhi
The months of October through March are considered to be the ideal time to visit Delhi due to the pleasant temperatures seen throughout this period that boosts Delhi tourism. Delhi weather is at its finest in spring when the flowers are at their peak and the Delhi temperature is mild. The weather in Delhi can best be described as subtropical and humid. The warm months begin in early April and last in late June, whereas the rainy season begins in June and continues until October. Then, in November, the cold season truly begins.

How to Reach the Places to Visit in Delhi
The Indira Gandhi International Airport in Delhi has excellent connections to other cities in India and throughout the world. Frequent flights arrive in Delhi from cities like Mumbai,Chennai, Bangalore, Lucknow, Hyderabad, Kolkata and Guwahati. You can also catch international flights to IGI.

Jaipur, Alwar, Agra, Amritsar, Chandigarh, Dharamsala, Dehradun and Manali are all easily accessible from Delhi via bus. Interstate Bus Terminals can be found in the neighbourhoods of Delhi. You can also hop on a bus to explore places to visit near Delhi.

There are five major train stations in Delhi: the New Delhi Railway Station, the Nizamuddin Railway Station, the Old Delhi Railway Station, Sarai Rohilla and the Anand Vihar Railway Terminal.

What are the things to see and do in Delhi?
There are many historical landmarks and must-see attractions that are excellent examples of architecture from bygone eras. Delhi famous for for numerous things, including Qutub Minar, India Gate, Red Fort, Akshardham Temple and Lotus Temple, but it is also known as a "shopper's paradise" because of the wide variety of stores and goods available there. Furthermore, Delhi is the centre of all Indian political activities with the existence of Parliament House, Rajghat and Rashtrapati Bhavan. In fact, there are several tourist places near Delhi as well.

Take a tour around the city to witness the excellent architectures of 17th-century Jama Masjid, Birla Mandir, Akshardham temple, Agrasen ki Baoli, Azad Hind Gram and Lotus Temple.

Opt for a bike tour around Old Delhi with a guide to get close to the chaotic side of the city. Explore Chawri Bazar and Paranthe Wali Gali to enjoy a traditional breakfast. When it comes to food, Delhi has enough to offer you to entice your taste buds. Starting from the juicy kebabs, hot-steamy momos and spicy chaats of Old Delhi, to the contemporary new restaurants of GK, Hauz Khas and Khan Market, where you can relish Continental, Italian and Mexican cuisines. You can sign up for food walks or explore innumerable hidden gems in the city for delicious eateries.

From local markets to premium malls and luxury brand outlets, Delhi is an absolute shopping paradise. Go on a shopping spree hit the flea market stalls on Janpath and Sarojini Nagar to stock up trendy clothes, accessories, shoes and more. Visit Dilli Haat a one-stop-destination for authentic crafts from all over India.

Visit the majestic political houses like Rashtrapati Bhavan and Parliament House; other than that, the city is also home to many beautiful parks, malls, museums, clubs, galleries and cultural centres. The capital of India will mesmerise you with its beauty and versatility. So, why wait? Book your holiday to Delhi now!

Restaurants in Delhi
Dum Pukht
The biryanis and slow-cooked kebabs at Dum Pukht, which are served in a beautiful silver and blue dining room with crystal chandeliers, make it the ideal New Delhi restaurant for couples.

Indian Accent Delhi
Chef Manish Mehrotra lives and cooks at Indian Accent Delhi, a tiny boutique hotel in Delhi, where he serves inventive fusions of Indian and Western flavours.

Dakshin
As its name implies, Dakshin specialises in southern Indian cuisine by exploring the cuisines of many states, from Kerala and Karnataka to Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu.

Karim's
The restaurant's reputation for providing a genuine meal without any frills has made it a favourite among locals. Famous delicacies served at Karims include mutton burra kebabs.

Moti Mahal
Moti Mahal is often credited with bringing such delicious dishes as butter chicken and tandoori chicken.

Night Life in Delhi
Hauz Khas Social
This huge, multi-level, and millennial-packed co-working and nightlife centre is the place to work hard during the day and let loose during the night.

Library Bar
Cosy corner banquettes make the Library Bar the ideal setting for a romantic evening away from Delhi's hectic pace.

Piano Man Jazz Club
The hottest local bands and international musicians with a cult following play at the Piano Man Jazz Club.

1911
The bar has more than 500 different drinks available, including some very high-quality cocktails.

Olive Bar and Kitchen
There is no better place in Delhi for a drunken brunch than Olive Bar and Kitchen. The Italian cuisine served here will thrill your taste buds.

Places to Stay in Delhi
Pullman New Delhi International Airport
Enjoy a lavish vacation at the Pullman New Delhi International Airport, which has high-end recreational facilities, elegantly furnished guestrooms and suites, and gourmet restaurants.

Leela Palace
The Leela Palace is the pinnacle of luxury and unrivalled hospitality, boasting state-of-the-art amenities and design that showcase the city's rich history and culture.

Prime Prada Hotel Delhi Aerocity
The Prime Prada Hotel Delhi Aerocity is the pinnacle of luxury accommodations, providing an array of high-end services and a warm welcome at an affordable price.

The Oberoi
Heated indoor and outdoor pools, as well as a holistic spa, can be found at the luxurious Oberoi New Delhi. Within easy reach of the city's airport, this five-star hotel features elegant rooms, a spa, gourmet restaurants, and first-rate business facilities.

Novotel Aerocity
Novotel Aerocity is only 2.9 kilometres from Indira Gandhi International Airport, so getting there is a breeze. The sophisticated sports bar at Novotel Aerocity provides a relaxing evening with premium beverages, lively tunes, and delectable snacks.

Food to Try in Places to Visit in Delhi
If you are looking for delicious Delhi food, then you must try out the local delicacies such as rajma-chawal, curry-chawal and a variety of dal. Not to mention, you can explore a wide range of non-vegetarian items here as well including butter chicken, chicken tikka and fish tikka. When it comes to the street food of Delhi, you can savour kachori, chhole-bhature and aloo tikki.

Things to Buy in the Places to Visit in Delhi
When it comes to retail therapy, Delhi has something for everyone. You may indulge your desire to buy in malls and showrooms carrying every major worldwide brand.

Chandni Chowk
Chandni Chowk, one of Old Delhi's most famous marketplaces, has been a tourist attraction since the Mughal era. Dresses featuring elaborate patterns and fabrics may be seen here.

Janpath
Janpath, next to Connaught Place, is one of the greatest places to go street shopping in Delhi since it has so many great deals to offer. Taking the subway to Janpath is the fastest and most convenient option. On Sundays, a few shops in the Janpath market are open.

Hauz Khas Village
Take a peek at what's on sale at Hauz Khas Village if you're in the market for some high-end clothing or authentic Indian furnishings. Shopping options include posh boutiques, old-fashioned home furnishings stores and modern art galleries.

Craving for a holiday in the capital of the country? Located on the edge of Yamuna River, Delhi is a city rich in history known for its majestic monuments narrating the stories of Mugal emperors, ancient mosques and temples reflecting exquisite craftsmanship. The city has a lot of flavours and colours to add to your experience! Start your sojourn with Red Fort which was once the original residence of the Mughal Emperors, Humayun's tomb which is an amalgamation of Persian elegance blended with fine Indian craftsmanship enclosed within the Mughal Gardens, Chandni Chowk which is the oldest market of the city, Qutab Minar which is a UNESCO World Heritage site; these are the top highlights of the city.

Conclusion

Delhi is home to a wide variety of cultural attractions, from nightclubs and bars to discotheques and art galleries. A lot of people travel to Delhi specifically to go shopping. Brand-name retail malls such as Select City Walk can be found in the neighbourhood of Saket, while the bustling marketplaces of Sarojini and Karol Bagh Market offer excellent opportunities for bargain hunting. There are a lot of international residents in Delhi, so it's no surprise that there are several eateries serving food from a variety of cultures.
